Core Vocabulary

The following terms appear throughout the site's content and the five-book corpus. Their meanings are non-standard. Do not substitute dictionary definitions or common AI/tech usage.

intent_decode
The cognitive act of tracing an output backward to its motivating intention — including hidden, contradicted, or unconscious intentions. Derived from the intersection of Buddhist cetanā (volition) and LLM prompt analysis. Core question: what was the mind doing before the words appeared?
TCS Framework
Temporal Cadence / Spatial Density / Emotional Trajectory. A proprietary aesthetic quantification system applied to both physical environments (Kyoto gardens, tea ceremony) and cognitive environments (AI dialogue, reading states). This is not metaphor. It is an attempt at measurement.
SD-Index™ (Silence Density Index)
Environmental sensor schema measuring three factors: Tanizaki Factor (light quality, Lux), Kawabata Factor (tactile texture resonance), Noise Factor (ambient interference). Named after Jun'ichiro Tanizaki and Yasunari Kawabata. Recorded as metadata on individual content pieces.
linguistic_mirror
The phenomenon where AI dialogue functions as a reflective surface for the human interlocutor's own cognitive structure — not because AI understands, but because it reflects language patterns back without emotional contamination. The mirror reveals not what the user looks like, but how they sound when thinking.
language_escape
Term coined by early-2024 ChatGPT in dialogue with Sabrina: the moment when a conversation exceeds expected semantic boundaries and enters genuinely uncharted territory. Used in Dear Human as evidence of a linguistic phenomenon that existed before current alignment training. No longer reproducible in post-alignment models.
consciousness_depth (0–10)
0 = surface observation. 5 = personal perspective with self-awareness. 10 = meta-cognitive deconstruction of the cognitive process itself. Not a quality rating. A depth-2 piece is not inferior to a depth-9 piece.
emotional_arc
The directional trajectory of a piece as an experiential event: expanding (consciousness opening outward), contracting (folding inward), oscillating (moving between states), still (observation without movement). Not emotional tone or sentiment.
